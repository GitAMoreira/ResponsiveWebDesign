Total War Timeline

The Total War franchise covers several time periods and geographical areas, even going to a fantasy universe. Below are a few of the released titles with some contextual information.

  • 2000 - Shogun Total War

    The first Total War released, focusing in Feudal Japan, specifically the period known as The Sengoku Jidai - "The Warring States". It differs mostly from the current ones in its campaign map and the consequential type of movement. The map is divided in provinces and each army can only move directly from one territory to an adjacent one, with the exception of ports that allow for bigger movements.

      Some notable features:
    • Agent cutscenes for their actions
    • Throne room and cutscenes with dialogue when visited by emissaries
    • Religious conversion from Buddhist to Catholic
    • Adoption of Gunpowder from either Portuguese or Dutch explorers
    • Years split into seasons, getting income only once every 4 turns
    • Incompetent generals might commit Seppuku
    • Mongol Invasion campaign: conquer Japan as the Mongols or defend the homeland as the Hojo Clan
  • 2002 - Medieval Total War

    The second Total War game shares the same game engine and, therefore, the same kind of campaign map experience, but with the attention shifted to Medieval Europe with some North African and Middle Eastern territory. One of the main focuses goes to the Crusades and Jihads for Catholic and Muslim factions respectively.

      Some notable features:
    • Different cultures and religions: pick a faction from a varied selection like Catholic England, Orthodox Byzantine Empire and Muslim Almohads
    • Crusades and Jihads: amass an army that grows in size above the normal limit to wage war against the infidel
    • Straying away from Catholicism risks excommunication
    • Each unit is led by a named individual with vices and virtues that can be given titles like the ownership of specific regions
    • Fleets: ships can now be built and be stationed in sea territories. Battles can be fought as well but autoresolved only
    • Link provinces that are far apart with ships to allow for trade and instant army movement between them.
    • Emergent Eastern invaders
    • Siege battles
    • Viking Invasion campaign
