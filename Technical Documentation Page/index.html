What is Steam

Steam is an online store for the digital distribution of videogames that is owned and ran by Valve Corporation. It works as a library of said videogames for each user. Steam also includes social network features dedicated to gaming in general with forums and community pages dedicated to each game. Other features exist, such as collecting trading cards in order to craft badges to show off on the user's profile, for example.

Store

Store is the place where you can do all of your purchases and browse through every title available at the moment. The Store contains 6 tabs, with the main one being the default, called "Featured". It shows the main Steam page when you open the app. The tabs are as follows:

  • Featured
  • Discovery Queue
  • Wishlist
  • Points Shop
  • News
  • Stats
Library

Library contains all of your purchased games and non-purchased, like Free To Play games that you didn't, necessarily, spent money on. It is also where you access them, whether it is to download them or play them. It contains 3 tabs:

  • Home
  • Collections
  • Downloads
Community

Community is the more social-focused part of Steam. Even though each game has its own Community page, this specific one is more broad, showing content from all the games, mainly the ones you have recently played. It contains 5 tabs:

  • Home
  • Discussions
  • Workshop
  • Market
  • Broadcasts
Profile

The Profile section contains a lot of information pertaining to the account currently logged in. It used to be called "Profile" but now it is named after the actual nickname of the account in question. It has 8 tabs:

  • Activity
  • Profile
  • Friends
  • Groups
  • Content
  • Badges
  • Inventory
  • Steam Replay
Tips

Below are some examples that might help navigate Steam, along with a few tricks to use on Valve games.

While on any tab on Steam, press Ctrl + f and type in whatever keyword you want to find it on your current page. This is particularly useful if you have a lot of items on your wishlist and want to find some of a particular franchise for example.

Press Shift + Tab during a game to open the Steam Overlay, which lets you chat or browse the web without exiting the game.

You can add custom launch options to games by right-clicking the title in your Library, selecting Properties, and adding something like -fullscreen in the launch options box.

Adding -dx11 to a game's launch options can help resolve graphical issues or force a specific mode.

To enable the console in Source engine games, go to settings and enable the developer console, then press (usually) \ to open it. You can use commands like sv_cheats 1 or noclip for testing.
